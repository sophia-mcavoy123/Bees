Honey Bees

Thesis: Honey bees are necessary for the human population to be able to survive at the fullest carrying capacity for an area possible.

10 Facts About Bees:

  1. Their average lifespan is 5-6 weeks
  2. They communicate with eachother through dancing
  3. It is the only food that humans can eat
  4. The honey bee has been around for millions of years.
  5. A colony of bees consists of 20,000-60,000 honeybees and one queen.
  6. Worker honey bees are only female
  7. Worker female bees do all the work
  8. The average worker bee produces about 1/12th teaspoon of honey in their lifetime
  9. On average, A hive of bees will fly 90,000 miles
  10. A honey bee visits 50 to 100 flowers during a a trip to collect pollen for honey
Points of views on Honey Bees:
Honey Bees are needed: Honey Bees are Not Needed:

As you can see from the table above, there are many different opinions on the need to save bees. While I do understand this, I still believe that bees are necessary for the outdoor environment. I have been lucky enough to be able to work with these lovely creatures. At my old school, my class took care of two hives and had the responsibility ourselves to take care of the hive, keep it clean, and make sure no animals or bugs got into the hive. I feel that this experience was critical to my understanding of the hive itself and how it works. If you yourself ever have the chance to do this, I highly recommend that you do so. All of the bees work together in harmony, and they all have two main goals in mind: making honey and reproducing more bees. The beauty that these bees create with out even know it is amazing. With your help, we can create a bee-utiful environment by doing one simple thing, spray no or pollinator friendly pesticides! Do you think that you can do this?
